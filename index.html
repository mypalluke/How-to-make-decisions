Phase I

JEHOVAH HAS GIVEN US FREE WILL

Water is essential to life, without it you cannot survive long.

There is a difference between surviving and living a good life, wouldn’t you agree?

Can you survive on very poor quality water? Yes, you can. Under a circumstance where you had to choose to drink bad tasting water and death, we would probably choose to live. But there is some water that is completely undrinkable, if you drank it could actually speed up your death. Poisoned or polluted water, sea water.

The point is we need water to survive, but we need pure clean water to thrive.

It is similar with decisions. Making decisions is essential to life. In fact, that is a scriptural statement.

Turn with me to Deuteronomy 30:19. Notice what the creator puts before his servants.

Read Deuteronomy 30:19 19 I take the heavens and the earth as witnesses against you today that I have put life and death before you, the blessing and the curse; and you must choose life so that you may live, you and your descendants

You must choose.

Jehovah taught them what was right and wrong so they could understand. He never took away their free will.

Jehovah, the Creator, respects us enough to say: "You must choose." He already knows what is best for us; he wants us not only to live, but to thrive—and yet he doesn’t make us do anything. It really is our choice.

The situation is the same today as with the israelites. There were other ways to live. Other gods to worship. Jehovah wasn’t going to stop them from going that direction, but he warned them.

Imagine being with an experienced survivalist guide on a hike in the woods. He knows all the plants you can eat, he has all the tools for survival, and a filter for the water you find. Wouldn’t you ask for his help as you are walking along?

With Jehovah’s help, we can make wise decisions in every aspect of our life. Today we will see the equipment Jehovah gives to make wise choices, a four step process to make good decisions, and the importance of implementing those decisions. Im excited to share this with you.

Phase II

JEHOVAH HAS EQUIPPED US

To have good clean water, you need the right tools: You need a filtration system, you need to know how to use the system, so you need the manual, and you need a test kit to ensure the water is safe.

To make good decisions, Jehovah has equipped us with the tools to make good clean decisions. We have the Bible in its entirety, we have been endowed with thinking ability, and been given a conscience that when trained by bible principles can test out decisions instantly.

Jehovah doesn’t just drop off these tools to us and hope for the best, notice his interest in each and everyone of us at Isaiah 48:17

Read Isaiah 48:17 17 This is what Jehovah says, your Repurchaser, the Holy One of Israel: “I, Jehovah, am your God, The One teaching you to benefit yourself, The One guiding you in the way you should walk.

Jehovah is our teacher, he wants us to benefit ourselves. We have the manual to life at our disposal: The Bible.

If you bought a piece of high-end machinery, the first thing you’d do is check the manual. Any serious manual says in bold letters: "Read and fully understand all instructions before operating this device." Why? Because the manufacturer knows that if you just "wing it," you won't get the results they promised.

Jehovah doesn't want us to just "glance" at the Bible when we're in trouble; he wants us to understand the process beforehand. Imagine if you only looked at the manual after you or someone in your family got violently ill from the water you drank. By then, the damage is done.

So what must we do? We should make it a goal to read his word everyday.

But we shouldn’t just read it. We must also think deeply on it.

Notice the Psalmist's secret in Psalm 119:97 and 100:

"How I do love your law! I ponder over it all day long... I act with more understanding than older men, Because I observe your orders."

He had more understanding because he didn't just know the orders—he pondered them. What does it mean to ponder over something?

The Law Example: The Law said: "You must not murder." Point-blank command. But using thinking ability, we see the source: Hatred. Meditating on irritating qualities until you can't stand to be in the same room. This is why Jesus said in Matthew 5:22 that even "continuing wrathful" or "words of contempt" makes us accountable. Make things right. You cannot worship Jehovah whom you cannot see, if you hate your brother whom you can see.

In the bible we are given “case studies”—records written as a warning to us. (1 Cor. 10:11). So we read Gods word, we ponder, and our conscience is trained.

Even the best filtration system won't work if the sensors aren't calibrated. Our conscience is that "hardware." We calibrate it using "solid food"—the deeper principles of the Bible. (Hebrews 5:14).

We realize we are equipped with these powerful tools. Now we just need to know how to put them into action. Following these bible based steps is similar to a water filtration system.

Phase III

FOLLOW STEPS TO A GOOD DECISION

Jehovah has given us a four-step purification process. This could be our decision filtration system. It’s a powerful metal tool that takes a muddy, high-pressure situation and refines it.

Step 1: Don’t be hasty (stop! think!).

Based on Pr 21:5: “The plans of the diligent surely lead to success...” In a water filter, the first stage is often a sediment trap. If you force muddy water through a fine filter immediately, you’ll clog the whole system. Spiritually, our "mud" is our raw emotions—panic, pride, or pressure.

Read Deuteronomy 32:29 29 If only they were wise! They would ponder over this. They would think about their outcome.

By being patient, we let the "mud" settle so we can actually see the situation for what it is. If you rush, you overlook important information. Do not make decisions based solely on your emotions. (Prov. 28:26; Jer. 17:9).

Step 2: Pray for wisdom.

Once water looks clear, we use a UV Light. It disrupts the DNA of invisible bacteria. This cannot be effective when the water is cloudy. When we pray as James 1:5 suggests, we are asking Jehovah for His light to "sanitize" our thinking from invisible "spiritual viruses"—subtle worldly philosophies.

James 1:5 5 So if any one of you is lacking in wisdom, let him keep asking God, for he gives generously...
Step 3: Do thorough research.

This is high-pressure filtration, like a fine membrane. This is the hard work. The answers are there. Proverbs 2:3-5 shows us:

"3 Moreover, if you call out for understanding... 4 If you keep seeking for it as for silver... 5 Then you will understand the fear of Jehovah..."

We separate human thinking from Jehovah’s wisdom. We use congregation tools and mature christians, but avoid asking “what would you do?” The only one we can throw our burdens on is Jehovah.

Step 4: Analyze your motives.

Final inspection in a small glass. How does it look? Smell? Taste? Jehovah wants us to be honest. Pr 16:2: "All of a man’s ways seem right to him, But Jehovah examines the motives." If we aren't sincere, the decision may not stick.

Example: Young brother pioneering realizes his "taste" is off—he wanted to please his parents. He recirculates. He goes back to Stage 1, prays, researches. Renewed with vigor and genuine love for Jehovah.

Warning: Avoid the Feedback Loop of ruminating. If you don't decide, a decision will be made for you.

Phase IV

IMPLEMENT YOUR DECISION

A purified decision is useless if you never “drink the water”. You could perish in as little as three days without water!

Don't get stuck in a "Feedback Loop." Paul said: "complete what you started" (2 Cor 8:10, 11).

Read Philippians 2:13 13 For God is the one who for the sake of his good pleasure energizes you, giving you both the desire and the power to act.

Jehovah gives you the "energy to lift the glass." This doesn't mean the path will be easy. (Psalm 34:19). Some good decisions lead to great difficulties—think of our brothers in prison. Was that a bad decision? Of course not!

However, be realistic. As Ecclesiastes 3:6 says, there is "a time to give up as lost." If you hit a dead end, don't give up blindly—recirculate. Go through the four steps again.

Phase V

YOUR MOST IMPORTANT DECISION

Jesus set this before every one of us at Matthew 16:24: "If anyone wants to come after me, let him disown himself..." This is dedication and baptism.

Jesus said at Luke 14:28 to "calculate the expense" of building a tower. Go through the process: don't be hasty, pray, research, examine motives.

Read James 4:8, 17 "Draw close to God... Therefore, if someone knows how to do what is right and yet does not do it, it is a sin for him."

What is preventing you? Baptism? Trust? This "purification system" can help you finally implement the most important decision of your life.

Some decisions we never go back on. Dedication. Marriage. Numbers 30:2: "he must not violate his word."

Conclusion

Does it matter to Jehovah? Absolutely. Proverbs 27:11: "make my heart rejoice, So that I can make a reply to him who taunts me."

Jehovah owns everything. What could we possibly give Him? Our heart. The one thing He will never take by force.

The handle of the cup is in your hand.
How will you use it?
