Will Your Decision Lead to Success?

30 Minute Discourse

30:00 LEFT

I. Introduction

Water is a fundamental element of life. Without it, we would have nothing. Here in our mountains, we know that even the clearest stream can be deceptive. Someone inexperienced might be tempted to drink because he’s thirsty now; but a wise person is cautious because they’ve seen the "runoff" of life.

We all face decisions that look clear on the surface but carry hidden risks.

The Young: Are choosing their friends, their education, or how they use their time online.

The Middle-Aged: Are balancing the pressures of work, raising a family, and caring for parents.

The Older: Are navigating health changes, deciding how to simplify their lives to focus on what matters most, or how to use their wealth of experience to encourage others.

In every stage, we ask the same question: Which decision leads to success?

Jehovah hasn't created us to be robots. He has given us the Dignity of Choice. He doesn't force us to drink the "Pure Water" of his wisdom, but he does invite us. When we stand at a crossroads, we often ask "What should I do?" But is there a more important question we should ask first? Notice the choice Jehovah puts before each and every one of us at Deuteronomy 30:19.

Read Deuteronomy 30:19

"I take the heavens and the earth as witnesses against you today that I have put life and death before you, the blessing and the curse; and you must choose life so that you may live, you and your descendants."

Jehovah, the Creator, respects us enough to say: "You must choose." He already knows what is best for us; he wants us not only to live, but to thrive—and yet he doesn’t make us do anything. It really is our choice.

Really, the question that we need to ask first is: What should guide my decision?

Illustrate: Imagine being with an experienced survivalist guide on a hike in the woods. He knows all the plants you can eat, he has all the tools for survival, and a filter for the water you find. Wouldn’t you ask for his help as you are walking along?

Jehovah provides that "filter" to help us see clearly. Whether you are making your first big decision or your five-hundredth, the process is the same. With his help, we can learn to make decisions that don't just feel good in the moment, but lead to a life of success.

27:00 LEFT

II. Equipped for Success

Visual: We look at this picture and we think, which path to choose. Interestingly in the picture both roads are narrow. Both are challenging. Isn’t that how life seems sometimes? Its not that you know for sure from the beginning which path will lead to success or not. Its difficult.

In the world we live in today, everyone is looking for an "Easy Button." People are all to willing to offer you advice on which path to choose. It’s tempting to just “push the button”, listen to them and hope for the best.

But here is the reality: that easy button always comes with a cost. You see, in life, we either make our own decisions, or they will be made for us. If we choose to follow the path of least resistance—letting our peers, our social media feed, or even our own raw impulses decide for us—we are at the mercy of the results. We are essentially drinking from whatever stream the world leads us to, without checking the source. Jehovah has equipped us to be the captain of our own ship.

If you bought a piece of high-end machinery, the first thing you’d do is check the manual. Jehovah doesn't want us to just "glance" at the Bible when we're in trouble; he wants us to understand the process beforehand. Imagine if you had a new water filtration system, but you only looked at the manual after you or someone in your family got violently ill? By then, the damage is done.

Refer to Psalm 119:97, 100

"How I do love your law! I ponder over it all day long. I act with more understanding than older men, Because I observe your orders."

That Psalmist studied the "Source Code" of God’s thinking. Let’s use a simple example: "You must not murder." Direct command. But what leads to murder? Hatred. What leads to hatred? A meditation of irritating qualities that leads to contempt.

Read Matthew 5:22 (Jesus' insight)

“However, I say to you that everyone who continues wrathful with his brother will be accountable to the court of justice... whereas whoever says, ‘You despicable fool!’ will be liable to the fiery Gehenna.”

That is thinking ability. As Isaiah 48:17 says, Jehovah’s guidance is "beneficial." When we understand the reason behind the laws, we are owning our decision.

Internal Hardware: Proverbs 2:10, 11 speak of "thinking ability." The most important sensor is your conscience—the "Sight Glass" on a filter. But a sensor only works if it’s calibrated. Hebrews 5:14 says our perceptive powers must be "trained through use."

20:00 LEFT

III. The Filtration System

When you’re standing in front of a mountain stream, you don't just hope the water is clean—you use your filter. Jehovah has given us a four-step purification process.

Step 1: Don’t be hasty.

In a physical water filter, the first stage is often a sediment trap. It relies on gravity and time. Our "mud" is often our raw emotions—panic, pride, or the pressure to act now.

Deuteronomy 32:29

"If only they were wise! They would ponder over this. They would think about their outcome."

Step 2: Pray for wisdom.

Once the water looks clear, we use a UV Light. UV light disrupts the DNA of invisible bacteria. This cannot be effective when the water is cloudy. When we pray as James 1:5 suggests, we are asking Jehovah for His light to "sanitize" our thinking.

Step 3: Do thorough research.

This is the stage of high-pressure filtration, like a fine membrane. It forces the water through a barrier so microscopic that only the pure water molecules pass.

Proverbs 2:3-5

"If you keep seeking for it as for silver... searching for it as for hidden treasures; Then you will understand the fear of Jehovah."

Step 4: Analyze your motives.

Finally, you pour a little into a small glass for a final inspection. How does it taste? Jehovah examines the motives (Prov 16:2).

Example: A young brother decides to pioneer but loses his joy. He realized his motive was to please parents. He doesn't quit; he recirculates. He goes back to Stage 1, prays, researches, and finds a genuine love for Jehovah as his new motive.

10:00 LEFT

IV. Implementation

Now we have a decision made—it’s a good, purified decision. Now is the time for implementation. It would be a tragedy to do all that work and then die of thirst because you never actually drank.

Read 2 Corinthians 8:10, 11

"...So now, also complete what you started to do, so that your readiness to act may be completed according to the means you have available."

Jehovah doesn't just give us the "water"; He gives us the energy to lift the glass. (Phil 2:13).

But does this mean a smooth path?

Psalm 34:19 says many are the hardships. Serving Jehovah can land someone in prison. Was that a bad decision? Of course not!

However, be realistic. As Ecclesiastes 3:6 says, there is a time to give up as lost. If a decision was beyond your ability, don't give up blindly. Recirculate. Go through the four steps again.

06:00 LEFT

V. The Most Important Decision

All of us here have a decision before us. Matthew 16:24: "If anyone wants to come after me, let him disown himself..." This is the decision to dedicate our lives to Jehovah.

Jesus said at Luke 14:28: "calculate the expense." Building a tower. Before making this life-altering decision, we shouldn't be hasty; we must pray, do research, and examine our motives.

Read James 4:8, 17

"Draw close to God, and he will draw close to you... Therefore, if someone knows how to do what is right and yet does not do it, it is a sin for him."

What is preventing you from taking that step toward baptism? This "purification system" can help you refine your thoughts so you can finally implement the most important decision of your life.

Some decisions we never go back on. Dedication vows. Marriage vows. Numbers 30:2 says "he must not violate his word."

02:00 LEFT

Conclusion

Does it really matter to Jehovah? Proverbs 27:11: "make my heart rejoice, So that I can make a reply to him who taunts me." Jehovah is keenly interested in you.

Jehovah owns everything. The mountains, the stars, the water. What could we possibly give Him?

We can give Him the one thing He will never take by force: Our heart.

We can choose to love Him. It is a dignity he respects. Now, the handle of the cup is in your hand. It is up to you to decide how to use it.
