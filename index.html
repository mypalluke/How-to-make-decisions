Will Your Decision Lead to Success?

30 Minute Discourse

30:00 LEFT

I. Introduction

Water is a fundamental element of life. Without it, we would have nothing. Here in our mountains, we know that even the clearest stream can be deceptive. Someone inexperienced might be tempted to drink because he’s thirsty now; but a wise person is cautious because they’ve seen the "runoff" of life.

We all face decisions that look clear on the surface but carry hidden risks.

The Young: Are choosing their friends, their education, or how they use their time online.

The Middle-Aged: Are balancing the pressures of work, raising a family, and caring for parents.

The Older: Are navigating health changes, deciding how to simplify their lives to focus on what matters most, or how to use their wealth of experience to encourage others.

In every stage, we ask the same question: Which decision leads to success?

Jehovah hasn't created us to be robots. He has given us the Dignity of Choice. He doesn't force us to drink the "Pure Water" of his wisdom, but he does invite us. When we stand at a crossroads, we often ask "What should I do?" But is there a more important question we should ask first? Notice the choice Jehovah puts before each and every one of us at Deuteronomy 30:19.

Read Deuteronomy 30:19

"I take the heavens and the earth as witnesses against you today that I have put life and death before you, the blessing and the curse; and you must choose life so that you may live, you and your descendants."

Jehovah, the Creator, respects us enough to say: "You must choose." He already knows what is best for us; he wants us not only to live, but to thrive—and yet he doesn’t make us do anything. It really is our choice.

Really, the question that we need to ask first is: What should guide my decision?

Illustrate: Imagine being with an experienced survivalist guide on a hike in the woods. He knows all the plants you can eat, he has all the tools for survival, and a filter for the water you find. Wouldn’t you ask for his help as you are walking along?

Jehovah provides that "filter" to help us see clearly. Whether you are making your first big decision or your five-hundredth, the process is the same. With his help, we can learn to make decisions that don't just feel good in the moment, but lead to a life of success. Let's look at how we can start that process.

27:00 LEFT

II. Equipped for Success

Visual: We look at this picture and we think: Which path should I choose? Interestingly, in the picture, both roads are narrow. Both look challenging. Isn’t that how life seems sometimes? It’s not always obvious from the start which path leads to success. It’s difficult.

But Jehovah gives us exactly what we need to choose correctly. Put simply, he gives us three things: His word the Bible, thinking ability, and our conscience.

If you bought a piece of high-end machinery for filtering water, the first thing you’d do is check the manual. Any serious manual says in bold letters: "Read and fully understand all instructions before operating this device." Why? Because the manufacturer knows that if you just "wing it," you won't get the results they promised.

Jehovah doesn't want us to just "glance" at the Bible when we're in trouble; he wants us to understand the process beforehand. Imagine if you only looked at the manual after you or someone in your family got violently ill from the water you drank? By then, the damage is done.

When a manufacturer includes a "Troubleshooting" section, is it because he wants to restrict you? No, it’s because he wants his machine to work perfectly for you. Notice how Jehovah describes his motive at Isaiah 48:17.

Read Isaiah 48:17

"I, Jehovah, am your God, The One teaching you to benefit yourself, The One guiding you in the way you should walk."

He is teaching you "for your own benefit." This isn't trial and error; it’s a filtration system designed by the One who made the water. When we apply his counsel, we are accepting a benefit he wants us to have.

In a world that prizes "life experience," the Psalmist made a bold claim: that there is a source of wisdom that surpasses the experience of older men. Let’s look at his secret in Psalm 119:97 and 100.

"How I do love your law! I ponder over it all day long... I act with more understanding than older men, Because I observe your orders."

He had more understanding because he didn't just know the orders—he pondered them. To "ponder" means to look at the "Source Code"—to understand why Jehovah gave the command in the first place.

Example: The Law said: "You must not murder." That is point-blank. But using thinking ability, we see the source: Hatred. Viewing someone as not worth living because of an opposing viewpoint. It starts with meditating on irritating qualities until you can't stand to be in the same room. This is why Jesus said in Matthew 5:22 that even "continuing wrathful" or "words of contempt" makes us accountable. By pondering, we found a principle that covers every aspect of life.

Every piece of equipment also comes with "test studies." In the Bible, we have the records of real people. 1 Corinthians 10:11 says their stories are "water test results." We see exactly what happened when someone bypassed the filter.

Finally, Jehovah endows us with the "Internal Hardware"—thinking ability and a conscience. Notice this "training program" at Proverbs 2:10, 11.

Refer to Proverbs 2:10, 11

"When wisdom enters your heart... Thinking ability will keep watch over you, And discernment will safeguard you."

Even the best filtration system won't work if the sensors aren't calibrated. Our conscience is that hardware. We calibrate it using "solid food"—the deeper principles of the Bible. (Heb 5:14). By comparing our small thoughts—like that tiny spark of irritation—to Jehovah’s thinking, our spiritual "sensors" become highly sensitive.

Transition: We realize we are equipped with these powerful tools. Now we just need to know how to put them into action. Following these bible based steps is similar to a water filtration system.
20:00 LEFT

III. The Filtration System

When you’re standing in front of a mountain stream, you don't just hope the water is clean—you use your filter. Jehovah has given us a four-step purification process. This is a powerful metal tool that takes a muddy, high-pressure situation and refines it until what is left is a clear, safe decision.

Step 1: Don’t be hasty (stop! think!).

In a physical filter, the first stage is often a sediment trap. It relies on gravity and time. If you try to force muddy water through a fine filter immediately, you’ll clog the whole system. Spiritually, our "mud" is often our raw emotions—panic, pride, or pressure.

Read Deuteronomy 32:29

"If only they were wise! They would ponder over this. They would think about their outcome."

A wise person "ponders over" the outcome. We let the emotions settle so we can see the situation clearly. This is not "wait and see"—it is diligently pondering. The Bible warns we cannot always trust our heart (Prov 28:26).

Step 2: Pray for wisdom.

Once the water looks clear, we use a UV Light. It disrupts the DNA of bacteria. This cannot be effective when the water is cloudy. When we pray as James 1:5 suggests, we are asking Jehovah for His light to sanitize our thinking.

James 1:5

"So if any one of you is lacking in wisdom, let him keep asking God, for he gives generously..."

Step 3: Do thorough research.

This is the stage of high-pressure filtration, like a fine membrane. It forces the water through a barrier so microscopic that only pure water passes.

Proverbs 2:3-5

"If you keep seeking for it as for silver... searching for it as for hidden treasures; Then you will understand the fear of Jehovah."

We are putting our thoughts under the "pressure" of God’s Word. This separates human thinking from Jehovah’s wisdom. We use congregation tools but avoid asking others “what would you do?” We must carry our own load.

Step 4: Analyze your motives.

Finally, you pour a little into a small glass. How does it smell? How does it taste? Jehovah examines the motives (Prov 16:2). It’s important to test if our decision is sincere, or it may not stick.

Example: A brother pioneering realizes the "taste" is off—he wanted to please his parents. He goes back to Stage 1. He prays. He researches. Finally, he finds a genuine love for Jehovah. Now he is no longer guided solely by emotion.

Warning: Avoid the Feedback Loop of second-guessing and ruminating. If you don't decide, a decision will be made for you.

10:00 LEFT

IV. Implementation

Now we have a decision made—it’s a good, purified decision. Now is the time for implementation. It’s time to act. It would be a tragedy to do all that work and then die of thirst because you never actually drank.

Read 2 Corinthians 8:10, 11

"And in this I give my opinion: This is for your benefit... So now, also complete what you started to do..."

Jehovah doesn't just give us the "water"; He gives us the energy to lift the glass. He gives us both the desire and the power to act. (Phil 2:13).

Does this mean a smooth path?

Psalm 34:19 says many are the hardships. Some good decisions lead to prison. Was that a bad decision? Of course not!

However, be realistic. If a decision was beyond your ability, as Ecclesiastes 3:6 says, there is a time to give up as lost. If you come to a dead end, recirculate. Go through the four-step process again.

06:00 LEFT

V. The Most Important Decision

All of us here have a decision before us. Matthew 16:24: "If anyone wants to come after me, let him disown himself..." This is the decision to dedicate our lives to Jehovah.

Jesus said at Luke 14:28: "calculate the expense." Building a tower. Before making this life-altering decision, we shouldn't be hasty; we must pray, do research, and examine our motives.

Read James 4:8, 17

"Draw close to God, and he will draw close to you... Therefore, if someone knows how to do what is right and yet does not do it, it is a sin for him."

What is preventing you from taking that step? This "purification system" can help you refine your thoughts so you can finally implement the most important decision of your life.

Some decisions we never go back on. Dedication vows. Marriage vows. Numbers 30:2 says "he must not violate his word."

02:00 LEFT

Conclusion

Does it really matter to Jehovah? Proverbs 27:11: "make my heart rejoice, So that I can make a reply to him who taunts me." Jehovah is keenly interested in you.

Jehovah owns everything. The mountains, the stars, the water. What could we possibly give Him?

We can give Him the one thing He will never take by force: Our heart.

We can choose to love Him. It is a dignity he respects. Now, the handle of the cup is in your hand. It is up to you to decide how to use it.
